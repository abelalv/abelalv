Spaces of vectors and matrices

A space vectorial \(V\) over a field \(F\) (it can be real or complex set) is a set with two operations: addition and scalar multiplication.

The opretations have the following properties:

  1. Closure: \(u,v \in V \Rightarrow u+v \in V\)
  2. Associativity: \(u,v,w \in V \Rightarrow (u+v)+w = u+(v+w)\)
  3. Commutativity: \(u,v \in V \Rightarrow u+v = v+u\)

  1. Identity: \(u \in V \Rightarrow u+0 = 0+u = u\)

  2. Inverse: \(u \in V \Rightarrow u+(-u) = (-u)+u = 0\)

  3. Distributivity: \(u,v,w \in V \Rightarrow a(u+v) = au+av\) \((u+v)w = uw+vw\)

  4. Distributivity by scalar multiplication: \(u \in V \Rightarrow (a+b)u = au+bu\)

Some examples of vector spaces are:

  • \(R^n\) the set of \(n\)-dimensional vectors with real components
  • \(C^n\) the set of \(n\)-dimensional vectors with complex components
  • \(R^{m\times n}\) the set of \(m\times n\) matrices with real components
  • polynomials of degree \(d\) in \(n\) variables
  • functions \(f:R^n \rightarrow R\)

Properties of vector spaces

Definition 1 (Linear combination) Let \(u,v \in V, a,b \in F \Rightarrow a u + b v \in V\)

  • Give a subset \(S\) of a vector space \(V\), the set of all linear combinations of elements of \(S\) is also a vector space. This set is called the span of \(S\) and is denoted by \(\text{span}(S)\).

Definition 2 (Subspace) The subset \(W\) of \(V\) is a subspace of \(V\) if \(W\) is a vector space.
